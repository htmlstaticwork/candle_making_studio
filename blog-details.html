Mastering the Perfect Scent Throw

8 Min Read | May 24, 2024 | Clara Meadows

The term "scent throw" refers to how well the fragrance of a candle travels through a space. In the candle-making world, we divide this into two categories: cold throw and hot throw.

Cold throw is the scent your candle gives off when it’s not lit. This is usually what people smell when they are browsing in a store. Hot throw, on the other hand, is the magic moment—the scent released as the wax melts and the fragrance oil evaporates into the air.

The Science of Temperature

The most common mistake new makers make is adding fragrance oil to wax that is either too hot or too cold. If the wax is too hot, you may "burn off" some of the lighter scent notes. If it's too cool, the oil won't bind properly with the wax molecules, leading to "bleeding" or poor throw later on.

For most soy waxes, the sweet spot is around 185°F (85°C). This allows the fragrance to fully integrate without degrading any of its delicate aromatic components.
